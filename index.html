1. Purpose

The purpose of this SOP is to provide a structured and standardized approach to managing biofouling for PLACEHOLDER COMPANY NAME. It links all related documents, including the Biofouling Management Plan (BFMP), Biofouling Record Book (BFRB), training and maintenance logs, and client communication materials. This SOP ensures compliance with the Australian Biosecurity Act 2015 and the Australian and New Zealand Anti-Fouling and In-Water Cleaning Guidelines (2023).

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all vessels under biosecurity control operated by PLACEHOLDER COMPANY NAME, as well as all personnel involved in biofouling management activities. It includes:

  • Vessel inspections
  • Biofouling cleaning and waste disposal
  • Maintenance of biofouling management equipment
  • Training and client communication

3. Regulatory Framework

This SOP is structured to comply with:

  • Australian Biosecurity Act 2015
  • Australian and New Zealand Anti-Fouling and In-Water Cleaning Guidelines (2023)
  • International Maritime Organization (IMO) Guidelines on biofouling

4. Roles and Responsibilities

Vessel Master: Oversees biofouling management activities, including inspections and cleaning, ensuring compliance with this SOP.

Environmental Officer: Manages all biofouling documentation (BFMP, BFRB), maintains record integrity, and ensures client communication materials are updated.

Maintenance Crew: Conducts inspections, cleaning, and equipment maintenance as per the SOP, adhering to documented standards.

All Crew Members: Participate in training, follow SOP procedures, and report biofouling incidents.

5. Biofouling Management Plan (BFMP)

The BFMP is a vessel-specific document that outlines biofouling inspection and cleaning routines. The plan should include inspection triggers, cleaning methods, and equipment used.

  • Inspection Triggers and Methods: See Section 7 for guidance on approved inspection and cleaning methods.
  • Documentation: Inspections and cleanings must be logged in the Biofouling Record Book (BFRB) (See Section 6).
  • Compliance: The BFMP must be reviewed annually to remain aligned with regulatory requirements.

6. Biofouling Record Book (BFRB)

The BFRB records all biofouling management activities. Each entry must include:

  • Date, activity type (inspection, cleaning), location, and findings.
  • Documentation of waste disposal, including volume and disposal method.
  • Record of any photographic evidence supporting the activity.

7. Inspection and Cleaning Procedures

Approved Inspection Methods: Visual inspections using ROVs or divers, with photographic records.

Approved Cleaning Methods: In-water cleaning using capture technology and approved filtration systems to prevent discharge into the marine environment.

8. Waste Management Procedures

Waste Capture and Disposal: All biofouling waste must be contained during cleaning activities and disposed of at approved facilities.

Containment and Transport: Waste is placed in secure bags and disposed of in compliance with local regulations.

9. Equipment Maintenance and Calibration

Equipment Maintenance: Routine inspections and repairs of filtration and capture systems.

Documentation: All maintenance activities are documented in the Equipment Maintenance Log.

10. Personnel Training Standards

Training includes operation of biofouling capture technology, waste disposal procedures, and accurate record-keeping in the BFRB.

11. Client Communication and Education

Client Education Materials: Provide clients with information about biofouling management requirements and benefits.

Communication Records: Document all client interactions related to biofouling management.

12. Continuous Improvement

Feedback Collection: A mechanism for collecting feedback to improve biofouling practices.

SOP Review: The SOP should be reviewed annually to remain aligned with updates in regulations and best practices.

  • Biosecurity Act 2015
